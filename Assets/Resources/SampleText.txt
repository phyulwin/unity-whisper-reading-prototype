Peter the fluffy Pomeranian woke up with a serious mission: today was grocery day.
He put on his tiniest red shopping scarf because he believed it made him look like a CEO.
At the market, Peter pushed a cart almost twice his size and pretended he was driving a race car.
He zipped past the apples, sniffed the bananas, and politely barked at a watermelon that seemed suspicious.
In the bread aisle, Peter chose the squishiest loaf and hugged it like a pillow.
He picked carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, and a bunch of parsley that tickled his nose until he sneezed.
A rolling onion escaped across the floor, and Peter chased it like it was the world championship ball.
Everyone laughed when he slid past the milk fridge and shouted, “Business is booming!”
At checkout, Peter stood very tall and placed one blueberry on the counter like a bonus executive item.
The cashier told him he was the cutest shopper in the entire company—I mean county.
Back home, Peter marched into the kitchen and announced that Chef Fluff was now in operation.
He washed the vegetables, though he accidentally gave one potato a bubble bath.
He stirred the soup with great importance, wearing a big apron that reached all the way to his paws.
When he tried to taste the sauce, a blob landed on his nose, and he looked like a very confused meatball.
Peter baked cheesy toast, chopped veggies, and mixed everything with a wiggle of his fluffy bottom.
The kitchen smelled so delicious that his family floated in behind the scent like cartoon detectives.
Mama, Papa, and his little sister Penny sat at the table while Peter carried out the meal with proud little steps.
They slurped the soup, crunched the toast, and declared it a five-star Pomeranian production.
Peter puffed up his fur, bowed deeply, and almost fell into the salad.
That night, his tummy was full, his family was happy, and Peter decided that shopping, cooking, and sharing were excellent for business.
